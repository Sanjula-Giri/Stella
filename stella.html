Inside a call

Four steps, every time — no phone tree, no hold music.

01

Answer

When something goes sideways

Calls don't hang in dead air, even when Stella gets stuck.

Every call runs with the same set of guardrails underneath it, regardless of business or vertical.

01

Silence timeout

If a caller goes quiet, Stella checks in once, then offers a human transfer rather than waiting forever.

02

Stuck-call detection

If the conversation is visibly going in circles, the call ends gracefully instead of looping.

03

Max call length

A duration cap keeps any single call from running away, with a clear handoff before the limit hits.

04

Automatic recovery

Brief hiccups in the voice connection are absorbed and retried so the caller never hears dead air.

Questions worth asking

Before you hand over your line.

Callers speak naturally and Stella responds in kind, including stopping mid-sentence the moment someone talks over it. Most callers don't realize until told.
It transfers to a real line or takes a detailed message with a callback promise — the moment a caller asks for a person, or after a couple of failed attempts to understand them.
Google Calendar is supported today, checked in real time before anything is booked. Businesses that don't take appointments — like a grocery store — can skip the calendar entirely.
Recording is optional per business, callers hear a consent notice up front where required, and retention windows are set independently for recordings, transcripts, and messages — shorter by default for sensitive calls.
Starting from the closest business template, most setups are ready to test within a few days and live within a week — off-hours first, then expanded once you've seen it handle real calls.
Trust & privacy

Built with the assumption that some calls carry real weight.

Recording, retention, and data handling are set per business — not a single blanket setting for every caller.

consent

Spoken up front

Callers hear a recording notice before anything is captured, matching two-party-consent requirements where they apply.

retention

Set per business

Recordings, transcripts, and messages each have their own retention window — shorter by default for sensitive calls.

review

Checked before go-live

Businesses handling health, legal, or financial calls go through a data-handling review before taking a single real call.
